# 4. Market-overview routine — what to check each morning

Europe's main cash markets (Euronext Brussels/Amsterdam/Paris, Xetra) open at
**09:00 CET** and Euronext closes at **17:30 CET**; the US opens at **15:30
CET**. So a Belgian investor's morning naturally splits into a European-open scan
and a US pre-market scan later.

Check, in order of importance:

- **Index levels & futures:** S&P 500 and Nasdaq (and their futures), Euro Stoxx
  50, plus your home indices **BEL 20, AEX, CAC 40, DAX**. Gauges overall risk
  appetite.
- **Volatility (VIX):** rising/elevated VIX = risk-off, tighten up and size
  down; low/falling VIX = calmer regime.
- **Bond yields:** US 10-year and the German Bund — rising yields pressure
  long-duration/growth stocks; falling yields support them.
- **EUR/USD:** matters for your US holdings (your returns are in euros; a falling
  dollar erodes US gains) and for European exporters.
- **Commodities:** oil, gold, gas, copper — relevant to energy/materials sectors
  and inflation.
- **Economic & earnings calendar:** ECB and Fed decisions, CPI/inflation, jobs
  (US non-farm payrolls), GDP — high-impact releases (typically flagged "three
  stars" on calendars) can dominate the tape; know what's due today and **avoid
  taking fresh risk right before a major print.** Note which of your holdings
  report earnings.
- **Pre-market movers & news:** biggest gappers/news and anything specific to
  your watchlist and holdings.

## Where to find it (free, retail-friendly)

- **Investing.com** — economic calendar, futures, indices.
- **TradingView** — charts, multi-asset watchlist.
- **Finviz** — US heatmap, movers, screener.
- **Yahoo Finance** — quotes, earnings/economic calendars.
- The live **Euronext** site for BEL 20/AEX/CAC quotes.
- **FRED** for VIX/yields history.
- **Bolero's in-app news** (ABM Nieuws, Reuters) plus its **"Beurs bij 't
  Ontbijt"** morning newsletter for a Belgian/European market briefing.
