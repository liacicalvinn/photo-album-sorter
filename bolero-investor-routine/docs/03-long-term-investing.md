# 3. Long-term investing methodology (buy-and-hold compounders)

## The master metric — ROIC vs. WACC

A business creates value only when its **return on invested capital (ROIC)
exceeds its weighted-average cost of capital (WACC)**, and the magic compounding
happens when it can *reinvest* large amounts of capital at that high spread.
ROIC is preferred to ROE because ROE can be inflated by leverage.

Look for **ROIC consistently above ~15%** (elite businesses sustain >20–25%),
evaluated as a **5–10-year trend**, not a single year, and relative to industry
peers.

## The wider quality checklist (the "Big Five" style screen)

ROIC, plus **revenue growth, EPS growth, equity (book value) growth, and
free-cash-flow growth** — ideally all ≥10% sustained over ~10 years. Confirm:

- **Free cash flow & conversion:** high ROIC should translate into real FCF
  (cash, not just accounting earnings) available for dividends, buybacks and
  reinvestment.
- **Margins & their trend** (gross, operating, net) — stable or rising indicates
  pricing power.
- **Balance sheet / debt:** manageable leverage and comfortable interest
  coverage so the business survives downturns (a common rule of thumb is **net
  debt/EBITDA below ~3×**, sector-dependent).
- **Moat:** brand, network effects, switching costs, scale, or regulatory/IP
  advantages (asset-light payment networks like Visa/Mastercard and software are
  classic high-ROIC examples). Beware mean reversion — most companies' ROIC
  fades toward average over a decade, so **durability is the key question.**
- **Management & capital allocation:** disciplined reinvestment, sensible
  buybacks, aligned incentives.
- **Valuation / margin of safety:** estimate intrinsic value (e.g. DCF of free
  cash flow over ~10 years plus terminal value; or compare P/E, PEG, P/FCF,
  EV/EBITDA to peers and history) and aim to buy at a meaningful discount
  (commonly a **15–30% margin of safety**). PEG (P/E ÷ growth) helps judge
  whether a high P/E is justified by growth.

## Screening criteria respected long-term investors use

- High and consistent **ROIC / ROCE (>15–20%)**.
- Positive and **growing FCF**.
- **Reasonable debt.**
- **Revenue / earnings growth.**
- A **valuation filter** (e.g. margin of safety >30%, or low P/FCF / PEG
  relative to growth).

Then do qualitative work on the **moat and management** before buying.
