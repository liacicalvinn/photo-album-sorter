# 7. Belgian / Bolero practicalities to bake into the routine

> **This is general information, not tax advice.** Confirm details with a Belgian
> tax professional — implementation of the 2026 regime is still settling.

- **TOB (transaction tax):** Bolero auto-withholds it — **0.35% on individual
  share buys *and* sells**. This is a direct drag on swing trading; factor
  ~**0.7% round-trip** into every trade's expected edge.

- **Capital-gains tax (new "solidarity contribution," from 1 Jan 2026):** a
  **10% flat tax** on realised gains on financial assets, adopted by the Chamber
  of Representatives in early April 2026 and retroactive to 1 January 2026.
  - The **first €10,000 per taxpayer per year is exempt** (€20,000 for married
    couples under community of property), indexed annually.
  - Unused exemption carried forward up to €1,000/yr to a maximum **€15,000 over
    five years**.
  - Gains accrued to **31 December 2025 are grandfathered** (listed assets valued
    at the last 2025 closing price).
  - Same-year losses are deductible.
  - There's also a separate risk that gains the tax authority deems
    **"speculative / abnormal management of a private estate"** are taxed as
    miscellaneous income at **33% (plus communal tax), with no base exemption and
    no historical-gain exemption** — the government having backtracked on
    removing this rule — another reason for a non-day-trading, thesis-driven
    approach.

- **Costs vs. size:** higher per-order fees on some European exchanges make very
  small or very frequent trades inefficient. **Prefer fewer, larger,
  higher-conviction positions.**
