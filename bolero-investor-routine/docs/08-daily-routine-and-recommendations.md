# 8. Daily routine & recommendations

## Stage 1 — Set the foundation (this week)

1. **Decide your split:** e.g. a passive world-ETF core (say 60–80% of the €35k)
   plus a satellite of individual long-term picks, and cap swing trading at a
   small slice (e.g. ≤10–15%, money you can afford to see swing).
2. **Define written rules:** per-swing-trade risk = 1% of capital (€350); max
   ~15–20 long-term positions; single-position cap ~7–10%; minimum
   reward-to-risk 2:1 for swings.
3. **Set up free tools:** a TradingView watchlist (your holdings + candidates +
   indices/VIX/EUR-USD/yields), Finviz for US movers, an Investing.com
   economic-calendar bookmark, and turn on Bolero price alerts + the morning
   newsletter.

## Stage 2 — Run the daily morning routine (≈30–45 min)

| Time | Step | Style |
|------|------|-------|
| **5 min** | **Market context** — indices/futures (S&P, Nasdaq, Euro Stoxx, BEL 20, AEX, CAC, DAX), VIX, EUR/USD, 10-yr yields, oil/gold; scan today's economic/earnings calendar. If a major release is due, plan to avoid fresh risk into it. | both |
| **5 min** | **News & holdings check** — Bolero/Reuters headlines + any news on your positions and watchlist; note earnings dates. | both |
| **10 min** | **Swing scan** — run your screen (above 50-day MA, RSI 40–60, tight base, volume dry-up, high RS, leading sector); review the 3–5 cleanest charts; for any candidate, write the exact entry, stop and target and check ≥2:1 and position size. Set alerts rather than chasing. | swing |
| **10 min** | **Long-term review** — no daily action needed most days. Periodically (weekly) advance one watchlist name through the quality checklist; act only when a quality name trades at a margin of safety. Check nothing in your thesis has broken. | long-term |
| **5–10 min** | **Decision & log** — place only pre-planned orders; journal what you did and why. **Most mornings the correct action is to do nothing.** | both |

## Stage 3 — Use the AI prompt before every buy

Paste the [Ultimate Stock Analysis Prompt](../prompts/ultimate-stock-analysis-prompt.md)
with the ticker; demand the reasoning and the bear case, then cross-check its
claims against primary data (Bolero/TradingView/filings) before acting — AI can
be wrong or out-of-date.

## Stage 4 — Review monthly / quarterly

- Recompute win rate and average R per swing setup (drop setups with profit
  factor <1).
- Rebalance long-term positions toward target weights.
- Harvest the €10k capital-gains exemption thoughtfully each year.
- Compare your total return honestly against a world-index ETF.

## Benchmarks that should change your behaviour

- If your **active sleeve (swings + picks) underperforms a world-index ETF over
  ~2–3 years** net of costs and taxes, shift more money to the passive core.
- If swing **win rate × average win isn't beating your costs** (commission +
  ~0.7% TOB round-trip), stop swing trading or widen timeframes.
- If a **single position exceeds your cap** or **VIX spikes** into a
  high-volatility regime, de-risk.

## Caveats

- **AI and web tools can be wrong or stale.** Always verify figures (ROIC, debt,
  valuation, earnings dates, price levels) against primary sources — company
  filings, Bolero, TradingView — before trading. Treat AI output as a structured
  first draft, not a decision.
- **Reddit is for frameworks and ideas, not signals.** Never trade a tip you
  haven't independently verified.
- **The retail base rate is sobering.** Most active retail investors underperform
  a simple index, often by 2–8 percentage points a year. The passive core
  protects you while you try to add value.
- **Costs and taxes are real drags in Belgium** — commission, ~0.35% TOB each
  way (and up to 1.32% TOB on certain accumulating ETFs), and the new 10%
  capital-gains regime from 2026 (with the 33% speculative-gains risk).
- **No guarantees and no get-rich-quick.** Markets fall, sometimes 30–50%, and
  individual stocks can go to zero. The €1m goal is realistic only over long
  horizons with consistent saving and compounding.
