# 2. Swing trading methodology (holding days to weeks)

## Trend & timeframe

Trade in the direction of the dominant trend. Use the **daily chart** as the
primary timeframe, the **weekly** for context. A simple trend filter many
traders use: only take long setups when price is above a **rising 50-day and
200-day moving average**. The "golden cross" (50-day crossing above the 200-day)
is a bullish regime signal; the reverse ("death cross") is bearish.

## Core indicators (don't overload — pick a few)

- **Moving averages (20 / 50 / 200-day):** trend direction and dynamic
  support/resistance. In uptrends, pullbacks to the 20- or 50-day often offer
  entries.
- **RSI (14):** momentum; <30 oversold, >70 overbought, but in strong trends RSI
  can stay extended. Many swing scans look for **RSI 40–60 on a pullback within
  an uptrend** (not overbought, not collapsing). Divergences (price new high,
  RSI not) warn of reversals.
- **MACD:** momentum shifts; bullish when MACD line crosses above signal line;
  histogram shrinking signals fading momentum.
- **Volume:** the deciding factor on breakouts — a breakout on volume **≥1.5–2×
  the 20-day average** is far more reliable than one on low volume (often a
  fake-out).
- **ATR:** for volatility-based stop placement (e.g. stop 1–1.5× ATR below entry
  so normal noise doesn't stop you out).
- **Relative strength (vs. index/sector):** prefer leaders, not laggards. An RS
  rank above ~80 is a desirable trait.

## What a quality setup looks like

Clear uptrend (price above rising 50/200-day MA), strong relative strength vs.
sector/index, a tight orderly base or continuation pattern (flag, triangle,
range) with volume drying up during the base, then expansion on the breakout, in
a sector that is leading.

**Quick mental checklist:** *RS strong, base tight, volume supportive, liquid
enough, aligned with market regime.*

## Entry / exit / stop rules

- **Entry:** on the breakout candle close above the base, or on a first pullback
  to the breakout level; require volume confirmation. For pullback setups, enter
  on a confirming signal (bullish engulfing/hammer, reclaim of the moving
  average).
- **Stop-loss:** below the base/recent swing low, or 1–1.5× ATR below entry.
  **Define it before entering.**
- **Target:** next resistance level or a measured move (height of the base added
  to the breakout); insist on **≥2:1 reward-to-risk**. If you can't find a
  logical stop that yields ≥2:1, **skip the trade.**
- **Management:** trail the stop higher as the trade works; consider taking
  partial profits into strength. Have a **time stop** — exit if the setup makes
  no progress after ~7–12 sessions.

## Sector rotation & scanning

Money rotates between sectors with the business cycle; track which sectors are
leading (relative strength vs. the index, or a Relative Rotation Graph). Trade
setups in leading sectors; avoid laggards.

**Morning swing scan:** filter for stocks above the 50-day MA, with RSI ~40–60,
forming a tight consolidation, on liquid names, then rank by relative strength
and review the cleanest charts.

## Reality check

Evidence consistently shows the most active retail traders tend to underperform;
bad timing and over-trading are the main culprits. In Belgium, each round-trip
also costs Bolero commission plus **0.35% TOB each way** and (from 2026)
potential **10% capital-gains tax** — so swing trading must clear a higher cost
hurdle than in the US. Keep it a minority of the portfolio and **journal every
trade.**
