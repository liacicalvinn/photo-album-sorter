# 5. Screening tools & data sources for a European retail investor

- **TradingView (free tier):** best all-round charts + multi-asset screener and
  watchlists; covers European + US markets. Free tier limits how many
  fundamental filters you can combine, but it covers ~80% of a European
  investor's needs; ~$15/month unlocks more.
- **Finviz (free):** excellent fast US screener (60+ fundamental + technical
  filters), heatmaps, movers; US-focused with minimal direct European coverage.
  Finviz Elite is $39.50/month (or $299.50/year) and adds real-time data, alerts
  and export.
- **Koyfin (free tier limited):** strong fundamentals, 500+ metrics and 10+ years
  of history; good for long-term analysis; free tier restricted (2 saved
  screens, ~2-year history).
- **ScreenerHero (free):** notable for free multi-criteria fundamental screening
  across European exchanges (Xetra, Euronext Paris/Amsterdam/Brussels, BME,
  Borsa Italiana and more) with no account required — useful where TradingView's
  free filters are capped.
- **Stockopedia / Simply Wall St / TIKR:** paid or freemium; Stockopedia (no
  free tier, ~€60–80/month) has strong quality/value rankings and good European
  coverage; Simply Wall St gives visual fundamental snapshots and intrinsic-value
  estimates; useful but not essential.
- **MarketScreener / Investing.com:** good for analyst consensus and breadth of
  coverage on European mid/small caps that bigger tools miss.
- **Bolero's own tools:** embedded screeners and shortlists, fundamental and
  technical analyses (partnerships with **Recognia** and **ChartMill**), analyst
  reports, and real-time quotes/alerts within the platform and app — convenient
  because it's where you actually trade.

## Suggested screen filters

### Long-term / quality screen

- ROIC > 15%
- FCF positive and growing
- Net debt/EBITDA < ~3 (sector-dependent)
- Revenue & EPS 5-yr growth > ~8–10%
- Gross/operating margins stable or rising
- Reasonable valuation (PEG < ~2 or P/FCF below peers)
- Market cap and average daily volume large enough for liquidity

Then **qualitative moat/management review.**

### Swing screen

- Price > 50-day MA (and ideally > 200-day)
- RSI 40–60 (pullback in uptrend) **or** new-high momentum
- Tight recent consolidation
- Volume dry-up in the base
- Relative strength rank high vs. index/sector
- Sufficient average daily volume
- In a leading sector
