# The Bolero Investor's Daily Research Routine & Ultimate Stock Analysis Prompt

A repeatable research process — not a jackpot machine — for a Belgian retail
investor running a ~€35,000 portfolio on **Bolero**. It pairs a disciplined
~30–45 minute morning routine with sound risk rules and a structured AI prompt
that forces a *pick + reasoning + bear case* before every trade.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general information and an educational process, **not
> personalised financial or tax advice**. Figures are historical/estimated, not
> promised. Markets fall, sometimes 30–50%, and individual stocks can go to
> zero. Verify every figure against primary sources (filings, Bolero,
> TradingView) before trading, and confirm Belgian tax specifics with a
> professional — the rules are still settling.

## TL;DR

- **Build a repeatable process, not a jackpot machine.** For a €35,000 portfolio
  on Bolero, the realistic edge comes from a disciplined ~30–45 minute morning
  routine, sound risk rules (risk ≤1–2% of capital per swing trade; ~12–20
  positions for long-term), and using the [AI prompt](prompts/ultimate-stock-analysis-prompt.md)
  to force a structured "pick + reasoning + bear case" before every trade.
  Growing €35k toward €1m is achievable but requires long timeframes: roughly
  36 years at a 10% net CAGR with no new money, or ~16 years at 10% if you add
  €1,000/month.
- **Two distinct playbooks, one routine.** Long-term buy-and-hold is driven by
  fundamentals (ROIC > WACC, durable moat, free cash flow, reasonable
  valuation / margin of safety); swing trading (days–weeks) is driven by
  technicals (trend above rising 50/200-day MA, RSI, MACD, volume-confirmed
  breakouts, relative strength, sector rotation) with pre-defined entry, stop and
  target giving ≥2:1 reward-to-risk.
- **Mind the Belgian-specific frictions.** Bolero auto-files the 0.35% TOB
  (transaction tax) on each stock buy and sell, and from 1 January 2026 a new
  10% "solidarity contribution" applies to financial-asset gains above a €10,000
  annual exemption per taxpayer. High per-trade costs plus these taxes make
  over-trading especially expensive — favour fewer, higher-conviction trades.

## Key principles

- **Reddit is for frameworks and ideas, not signals.** Predictive value of
  Reddit "buy" calls collapsed after the GameStop episode — mine it for
  checklists and watchlist ideas you verify yourself.
- **Risk management is the real differentiator.** Position sizing,
  diversification, stop discipline and realistic expectations matter more than
  stock selection. Beating a simple world-index ETF is a high bar — keep a
  passive core and treat active picking as a smaller, deliberate sleeve.
- **Costs and taxes are real drags in Belgium.** Commission + ~0.35% TOB each
  way (up to 1.32% on certain accumulating ETFs) + the 2026 capital-gains
  regime especially penalise frequent trading.
- **No get-rich-quick.** The €1m goal is realistic only over long horizons with
  consistent saving and compounding — not through high-risk trading.
